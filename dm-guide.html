How Events Work

Aethrya's Dungeon Masters are the custodians of the world's living story. They do not script events from first scene to last â€” they prepare narrative frameworks, NPC rosters, and consequence trees, then follow wherever player agency leads. No prepared scene survives full contact with a player character who has decided to do something unexpected, and the DM team considers this a feature rather than a problem.

Events fall into two categories. Reactive Events emerge organically from the world's ongoing storylines without requiring player initiation. Requested Events are submitted through the DM request channel, scheduled based on character mastery, request quality, and narrative fit with active arcs.

Requesting an Event

Submit DM event requests through #dm-requests in Discord. A strong request includes: the characters who should be present, a brief summary of the story thread you want to advance, the narrative question you want the event to engage with, and a genuine openness to outcomes you did not anticipate. Requests that specify a desired outcome in precise detail receive lower priority than requests that specify a desired question. The DM team schedules events within two weeks of approval.

Good: "Vaerin has been following the lead from the mercenary camp â€” I want an event where she finally meets the officer who gave the order. I'm open to anything, including it going badly."

Less good: "I want my character to find out who Aldric Brand really is and get proof they can share with the Veilbreakers."

During an Event

DM events follow standard Aethrya emote format. The DM plays NPCs and narrates environmental developments; players control only their own characters. Dice rolls are called by the DM and resolved publicly. Consequences established in a DM event are permanent unless the DM explicitly frames a consequence as reversible. Characters who die in DM events follow the standard permadeath procedure.
