Practical Disguise Rules

How disguises are constructed, registered, maintained, and penetrated — including the Veil Accord's special provisions for divine disguises that no mortal skill check was ever designed to unravel.

Mortal Disguises

Any character may attempt a disguise using Disguise Kit tools. Quality is determined by the proficiency check result and the complexity of what is being concealed — disguising as a different race requires a higher DC than disguising within your own. Disguising as a specific named individual (Impostoring) requires the Assassin Rogue's Impostor feature or equivalent magical capability. Penetrating a disguise uses Perception or Investigation against the disguiser's Deception check.

Divine Disguises▶

Divine disguises — the mortal forms maintained by Pantheon Race players and NPC deities — operate at a categorically different level than mortal disguise work. They are sustained by divine will in ways no Deception check can replicate, and they resist Dispel Magic, which interacts only with Weave-mediated effects. Divine disguises may be penetrated by: specific magical detection devices (Galdrick Sparkwit's prototype, currently functional at forty feet), certain high-tier True Seeing applications, and — most critically — accumulated behavioural evidence. The Veilbreakers use this last method almost exclusively.

Registered Aliases▶

Characters who use disguises regularly may register recurring aliases with the DM team. A registered alias has a name, consistent visual description, and documented social role. Registered aliases are treated as persistent entities in the world's information systems — hub city records, faction rosters, wanted notices. They create a second life for your character that the world treats as real, with all the rich complexity that implies.
