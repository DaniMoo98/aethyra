Pantheons

The Primordial Titans — Before the Gods

The ancient presences that predate every pantheon, every accord, every theological tradition in the known world — not gods in any recognisable sense, not bound by any covenant, and currently expressing themselves through geological events that four data points in the Grove Network's contamination map are beginning to make terrifyingly legible.

"The Veil Accord governs divine beings who operate within the mortal plane in ways mortals would classify as miraculous. The Primordial Titans do not operate. They exist. The Accord does not address what occurs when existence at that scale begins to express itself through the shape of the stone itself." — Silent Synod research paper, highest classification, two of three copies currently unaccounted for.

Overview

The Primordial Titans are the oldest divine presences in Aethrya — entities that predate the Faerûnian Pantheon's formation by millennia, predate the Seldarine's court structure, predate every organised theological tradition in the known world. They are not gods in any recognisable framework: they do not maintain mortal institutions, do not seek worship, and do not participate in the divine cold war's political architecture. They simply are — enormous, ancient, patient, and currently moving in ways that geological stability does not explain and that the gods watching from their mortal forms have not finished arguing about how to address.

The Veil Accord does not address the Primordial Titans. Whether this is because Ao determined they were outside the Accord's scope, because the gods negotiating could not agree what language to use for entities who operate at a scale that makes language feel approximate, or because acknowledging them in the Accord's text would require acknowledging what their existence implies about the nature of divinity itself — this remains the Silent Synod's most significant open research question, and the one they are most careful to discuss only in person.

The Tide-That-Remembers
The oceanic Primordial — associated with the deep sea and the things that have inhabited it since before mortal civilisation began naming things. Deep Sashelas's aquatic research operations have been documenting unusual oceanic behaviour patterns for sixty years. The patterns have been accelerating for three.
The Waking One
The terrestrial Primordial — whose proximity produces the contamination pattern the Grove Network is documenting. Its awakening is not a single event but a gradual, centuries-long increase in geological and ecological expression that Circle-Speaker Verdant's four data points are beginning to define. The shape forming in the data is not geological. It is intentional.
The Seventh
Referenced in the Silent Synod's oldest documents and in Primordial Titan-era texts held by Corellon's Preservation Circles. Not confirmed as currently active. The oldest records describe the Seventh's domain in terms that resist translation into any language currently in use — which is either a limitation of ancient translation or a property of the domain itself. The Synod has not reached consensus on which.

The Primordial Titan awakening is Aethrya's season-defining escalating pressure — present in Phandalin's seismic reports, in the Grove Network's contamination data, in the Zealot Barbarian's investigation thread, in the geological anomalies Rangers encounter beyond the frontier. When the pattern is identified and its shape is fully understood, what follows will not be an ending. It will be the beginning of something the divine cold war's current participants have no framework to address together. The gods are not ready. The mortals are certainly not ready. The characters that Aethrya's players have built together will decide, in the space between those two certainties, what happens when ready stops being an option.
