Magic & The Weave

Every arcane spell cast in Aethrya is drawn through a living divine framework maintained by a hidden goddess. Every divine spell is answered by a patron who is present in the mortal world, somewhere, in mortal form. Magic in Aethrya is never private. There is always someone on the other end.

Mystra is the Weave. The Church of the Seven Stars' registration system is not merely administrative — it is Tessara Wand's comprehensive census of every arcane talent in the Throne of Ashes. You are in her files. The question is which section.

The Spell Point System

Aethrya uses Spell Points rather than traditional spell slots. A character's SP pool is determined by their class at each mastery tier, recovering fully on Long Rest. Wizards also restore SP on Short Rest via Arcane Recovery; Warlocks recover all SP on Short Rest.

Arcane vs. Divine Magic

Arcane magic — Wizard, Sorcerer, Artificer — flows through the Weave. It is subject to the Weave's structural conditions and registered with Mystra's institutional census. Divine magic — Cleric, Paladin, Druid, Ranger — flows through the direct connection between mortal and deity. It is not Weave-dependent, but it is never truly private: the deity on the other end of the connection is always present, always aware, and always accumulating information about what their mortal network is doing.

The doctoral student at the Imperial College has, through research into arcane theory, approached the theoretical foundations of Weave-breaking — a discovery that would disrupt both systems simultaneously. Mystra has not yet decided what to do. Shar's representative was assigned to monitor the situation two weeks ago. Three other divine agents are watching the same student. The student is attending a seminar on theoretical arcane constants and has no idea.

Concentration

Concentration spells — spells that require maintained mental focus — end immediately if the caster casts another concentration spell or falls unconscious. Taking damage while concentrating requires a CON saving throw (DC 10 or half the damage dealt, whichever is higher) to maintain concentration. A character may never concentrate on more than one spell simultaneously. Managing concentration in complex encounters is one of Aethrya's most meaningful tactical challenges and one of the clearest expressions of the difference between a Novice and an Expert spellcaster under pressure.
