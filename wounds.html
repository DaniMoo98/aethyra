The Wound System

How wounds are applied, tracked, treated â€” and why a character who survived a fight with three grievous injuries tells a richer story than one who emerged unscathed. Physical conflict in Aethrya leaves marks, and those marks are part of the narrative.

The wound system exists to ensure combat carries consequences beyond Spell Point expenditure. A character who survives a difficult fight is not the same character who entered it. Wounds are not punishments â€” they are the body's record of what the character has been through, and they make every subsequent interaction richer for carrying that history.

How Wounds Are Applied

Wounds are applied when a character drops to 0 HP and is revived, takes damage from a critical hit above a threshold scaled to the character's HP maximum, or is targeted by a wound-inflicting ability, trap, or environmental hazard. Not every combat produces wounds â€” only consequential ones. DM events have DM confirmation; player-versus-player combat wounds require Moderation confirmation.

Wound Roleplay

Active wounds must be reflected in your character's emotes and scene behaviour. A character with a severe shoulder wound performing overhead strikes without acknowledging the limitation is not engaging with the system in good faith. Wounds are narrative assets â€” they give other characters legitimate hooks for care, concern, or tactical observation, and they make every scene in which they appear more textured than it would be without them.

The Monk's Wholeness of Body feature, the Cleric Life domain's healing capabilities, and the Artificer Alchemist's Restorative Reagents all interact specifically with the wound system in ways that give these options distinct value beyond raw HP numbers â€” a meaningful reward for characters whose builds support recovery as well as conflict.
