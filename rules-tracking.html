Tracking & Points of Interest

How wilderness tracking abilities function, and how Aethrya's Points of Interest are discovered, registered, and maintained â€” the cartography of a living world that rewards those who wander with purpose.

Tracking

Characters with Survival proficiency or the Favored Enemy feature may track creatures or characters through wilderness regions. Tracking checks use Wisdom (Survival) against a DC set by terrain difficulty, time elapsed since the target passed, and the target's active concealment efforts. A successful check reveals the trail's direction, approximate age, and the size of the group being tracked.

Characters actively concealing their trail use Dexterity (Stealth) to contest tracking attempts. Magical concealment â€” Pass Without Trace, Nondetection â€” provides specific DCs that standard tracking cannot exceed without equivalent magical assistance. A character who understands they are being tracked and who acts accordingly creates one of Aethrya's most compelling chase narratives.

Points of Interest

Points of Interest (POIs) are player-discovered or DM-placed locations carrying narrative significance: ancient ruins, divine artifact sites, Primordial Titan manifestation points, hidden caches, faction installations. POIs are registered with the DM team and tracked on the server's internal world map.

Players who discover a POI through legitimate IC exploration may claim discovery credit â€” acknowledged in the world as that character's historical achievement, potentially unlocking DM event hooks tied to the location. POIs without player visits within 30 days revert to undiscovered status and may be found again by anyone.

Primordial Titan manifestation sites are a special POI category under elevated DM monitoring. Characters who spend significant time at Titan sites will notice animals behaving abnormally, magical effects amplifying or dampening without cause, and some characters beginning to experience visions the DM team manages individually. Discovery of a new Titan site is among the most significant world events a player character can trigger.
