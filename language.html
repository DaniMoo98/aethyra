Languages of Aethrya

Common tongues, racial scripts, divine languages, and the forbidden utterances whose very use constitutes a detectable act under certain interpretations of the Veil Accord — the linguistic architecture of a world where words have always carried more weight than they appear to.

Common Languages

LanguageSpoken ByNotes
CommonAll races (trade standard)The lingua franca of the Throne of Ashes and all hub cities
ElvishElves, Half-ElvesThe Long Memory is transmitted in High Elvish script; spoken dialects vary by subrace
DwarvishDwarvesMountain and Gold Dwarf dialects are mutually intelligible; Duergar dialect is markedly distinct
HalflingHalflingsPrimarily oral tradition — rarely written. Yondalla's protection extends most strongly to its native speakers
GnomishGnomes, technical scholarsThe primary language of artificer documentation and engineering record-keeping throughout the empire
DraconicDragonborn, dragons, arcane scholarsThe oldest language in continuous use — predates the Veil Accord. Certain spells exist recorded only in Draconic
UndercommonDrow, Duergar, Underdark tradersThe trade pidgin of the deep places; carrying it on the surface marks a speaker as Underdark-affiliated
OrcOrcs, Half-Orcs, frontier peoplesAssociated with Gruumsh's ancient tradition; the language of Barbarian oral war-histories

Divine Scripts

Celestial
The script of the upper planes and divine bureaucracy. Required for Cleric and Paladin divine communication above Adept tier. Naturally readable by Aasimar.
Infernal
The script of the Nine Hells — used in Tiefling heritage documents, Warlock Fiend pact language, and Bane's Black Fist contract fine print. The provisions no one reads carefully enough.
Abyssal
The chaos-language of the Abyss. Associated with Maerath's demon-adjacent operations and certain Great Old One Warlock communications. Audibly disturbing when spoken aloud in enclosed spaces.
Primordial
The language of elemental forces — spoken natively by Genasi as inherited dialects. When used at Titan sites, it has effects that the DM team manages on a per-character basis. Use with deliberate caution.

Certain languages are classified as Forbidden Tongues under the Imperial Accord Faith's theological doctrine. Their use in public is prohibited; their ritual use attracts Judicator attention. Deep Speech — the language of Aberrations and certain Primordial entities — is the most frequently encountered forbidden tongue in Aethrya's current season arc, as its speakers increasingly overlap with characters investigating the Titan awakening pattern. The Judicators have not publicly addressed what it means that more people seem to be learning it.
