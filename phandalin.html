Phandalin — The Frontier's Edge

The furthest permanent settlement into the unmapped Veillands — rebuilt twice, fortified against a threat it has never fully been able to name, and increasingly aware that the geological reports its expedition parties bring back describe something no existing model can explain.

Overview

Phandalin has been destroyed and rebuilt twice in the last two centuries. It endures because the Wave Echo Cave system at its centre — source of the ore that has funded every reconstruction — is too valuable to abandon. The current settlement is the most fortified iteration yet: stone-walled, approximately four hundred permanent residents, with a rotating population of expedition personnel, researchers, and the particular breed of fortune-hunter who prefers honest frontier odds to Leilon's calculated ones.

Phandalin operates under partial hub status — its central market district follows hub city conduct rules, while the rest of the town and all territory beyond its walls follows standard frontier rules. This creates a distinctive social gradient: civilised norms from the market square inward, increasingly flexible standards as you approach the gate, and beyond the gate, whatever the wilderness chooses to be that day.

The Tresendar Estate
The town's administrative centre, repurposed from an old manor. Town Master Harlan Wester maintains the expedition ledger here — the official record of every party that has gone into the Veillands and what they reported back. The ledger has seventeen entries describing the same anomalous locations in seventeen different ways.
Wave Echo Cave
The mine that justifies the town. Currently at 60% capacity following a seismic event that collapsed two lower shafts three months ago. The miners who were working those sections say the stone moved in ways that were not geological. Wester has written this down. He has not yet decided who to tell.
The Stonehill Inn
Phandalin's largest public house and the de facto information exchange. Expedition parties debrief here over drinks that are stronger than they appear. The board near the door holds an ever-changing collection of maps, wanted notices, and commission requests from people who prefer not to give their names.
The Shrine of Luck
A modest but immaculately maintained Tymoran shrine. Its halfling priest Linene Graywind has sent at least a dozen expedition parties into the Veillands with a sermon on bold action. Three of them came back with extraordinary findings. She counts this as evidence. Kessa Brightfall has visited twice.
