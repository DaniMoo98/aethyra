Pantheons

The Faerûnian Pantheon — Full Roster

Not a unified church but a coalition of sovereign deities with overlapping domains, individual theological traditions, and a four-thousand-year history of rivalry conducted through the most elaborate system of institutional proxies ever assembled. Every Faerûnian institution you trust is managed by a being you have never seen in their true form.

The Faerûnian Pantheon consolidated approximately six thousand years ago. Its foundational myth describes a voluntary association — gods recognising that human civilisation was the fastest-growing source of mortal belief and that coordinated influence was more efficient than unchecked competition. The Divine War dismantled their open rule. The Veil Accord forced them into their current mode: collective influence through competing institutions, disguised divine presence, and the patient management of human ignorance across a timescale no mortal civilisation can comprehend from within.

Three ancient texts contain the same structural gap at the same position in their deity lists. A fourth text with the identical gap was recently identified. Theologians have debated for centuries whether the Faerûnian Pantheon's missing member was deliberately excised from the historical record, was never canonised, or represents something the records were never permitted to describe accurately. The Silent Synod's file on this question is their oldest open case. It is also the case they discuss least frequently in written correspondence.
