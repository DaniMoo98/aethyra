Spells & Rituals

Master Tier Spells

Seventh, eighth, and ninth level spells — magic at the scale of legend, where each casting reshapes reality in ways the world cannot ignore and divine networks cannot afford to overlook. Master-tier spellcasting in Aethrya is not merely powerful. It is geopolitically significant.

Master-tier spell use is among the most observed magical activities in Aethrya. Mystra's Church registers every known caster who demonstrates this level of Weave access. Every divine faction that monitors magical activity has a protocol for when a mortal reaches this tier. The Judicators have a standing interest in any Master-tier casting that intersects with divine-adjacent affairs. You are, at this level, impossible to ignore — and the wise Master caster knows that being impossible to ignore is both a tremendous asset and a potentially terminal condition.

Simulacrum
IllusionMasterWizard
Create a duplicate of a creature from snow or ice, endowed with half their HP and capabilities. The duplicate has no independent will and obeys the caster's commands. In Aethrya, a Simulacrum of a divine mortal-form agent is the most legally and theologically complex object any caster could produce — and several divine factions have explicitly instructed their mortal networks to report any Simulacrum casting immediately to their chain of command, without explanation.
Wish
ConjurationMasterWizard · Sorcerer
The most powerful spell available to mortal spellcasters — capable of replicating any spell of 8th level or lower, or attempting effects beyond normal magical scope at the DM's determination. In Aethrya, Wish is managed as a DM event: the caster states their wish, and the DM determines its implementation in the context of the world's current narrative state. The wish-granting tradition's relationship to the Veil Accord is a matter of ongoing divine legal dispute that has never been formally resolved.
Meteor Swarm
EvocationMasterWizard · Sorcerer
Blazing orbs of fire plummet from the sky at four points you choose within one mile — 40d6 fire and bludgeoning damage each, 40-foot radius. The most destructive spell available to mortal casters. Its use constitutes a hub city violation at any tier. Its use in populated territory is a Consequence Rules escalation that the DM team manages as a world event. Two historical uses have left geological features that are still visible in the landscape. The casters' names are still in the record.
Foresight
DivinationMasterWizard · Druid · Bard · Warlock
For 8 hours, one creature you touch cannot be surprised, has advantage on attack rolls, ability checks, and saving throws, and all attacks against them have disadvantage. The definitive protective spell at Master tier — not through resistance but through awareness. Foresight is the spell that makes Master-tier diviners the most sought-after allies in any serious operation, and the one whose duration and passive nature makes it simultaneously the most valuable and least dramatic demonstration of what a Master diviner can provide.
Astral Projection
NecromancyMasterCleric · Monk · Warlock · Wizard
Project into the Astral Plane with up to eight willing creatures, leaving your physical body behind. In Aethrya, the Astral Plane is not neutral ground — it is the corridor through which divine communication between the hidden gods and their true divine forms passes. A mortal who enters the Astral Plane at Master tier is entering a space where the Veil Accord's provisions become unclear and where what they might observe is something the Accord's authors did not intend mortals to see. DM consultation required before this spell is cast in a narrative context.
