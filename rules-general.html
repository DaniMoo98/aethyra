Rules

General Rules

The baseline standards governing all conduct on Aethrya — the principles without which long-form collaborative roleplay cannot function, and the guarantees that make the world safe enough for genuine vulnerability in storytelling.

These rules apply to every player, character, and interaction without exception. They are not negotiated by in-character logic.

OOC Conduct▶

All out-of-character communication must be respectful. Disagreements about character actions are resolved through DM mediation, never OOC argument. Use (( )) to indicate OOC speech in scene channels and minimise interruptions during active scenes. The distinction between IC conflict and OOC hostility must be maintained at all times.

Discrimination & Harassment▶

Zero tolerance for real-world discrimination in OOC communication or in IC content that uses fantasy prejudice as a vehicle for real prejudice. Targeted character antagonism designed to drive a player away from the server — rather than serve genuine narrative purpose — constitutes harassment regardless of IC justification.

Metagaming▶

Characters may only act on information their character has legitimately acquired IC. OOC knowledge of divine identities, other characters' secrets, or server announcements may not inform character decisions. Divine identity metagaming — acting on OOC knowledge of a god's mortal form — is the most serious form of this violation and is treated accordingly.

Consent Framework▶

Active consent is required for scenes involving violence against player characters, romantic content, and significant psychological themes. The X Card pauses any scene instantly without explanation required. Consent given in one scene does not carry to subsequent scenes.

Powergaming▶

Prioritising mechanical advantage over narrative coherence, ignoring injuries the fiction demands, or exploiting rules against the story's internal logic is prohibited. The rules system exists to serve the narrative, not to be weaponised against it.

Character Death▶

Permadeath is possible through the death save system in DM-adjudicated combat. PvP death requires DM approval. "Permadeath without DM approval" in PvP contexts is prohibited. Death is a narrative event managed collaboratively — not an outcome imposed unilaterally.
