Pantheons

The Seldarine — The Elven Court

A court rather than a coalition — bound by kinship, shared history, and the Long Memory tradition that preserves theological truth across timescales no human institution survives intact. The Seldarine's divine cold war strategy is patience itself: elves remember what mortal civilisations forget, and in Aethrya, memory is the most dangerous weapon of all.

Corellon Larethian — The First Among Equals
God of Arts, Magic, and Elven Identity · Protector of the Preservation Circles
ArtsMagicElvesPre-Veil Knowledge

Corellon's Preservation Circles in Silvenmoor maintain libraries of pre-Veil knowledge — documentation of what the world looked like before gods walked in disguise. Four thousand years of careful divine archival management have not erased these records. The Circles know things three pantheons would prefer they didn't. Their continued, uninterrupted operation is Corellon's most defiant act in the current era, and one that several gods have repeatedly considered "addressing" before concluding that addressing it would confirm the very concerns the Circles have been documenting.

Mortal Form: An ancient Sun Elf archivist known as the Keeper, whose documented tenure in the Silvenmoor preservation complex extends back six hundred years without any record of aging. The Sun Elves regard this as evidence of exceptional divine blessing. It is, technically, accurate.
Aerdrie Faenya — The Winged Mother
Goddess of Air, Wind, and the Avariel
AirWindAviansLong-Distance Communication

Aerdrie Faenya's Windrunner network is Aethrya's most effective long-distance intelligence service — Avariel carrying information between separated communities with a speed ground-bound networks cannot match. The Windrunners are simultaneously a divine intelligence operation and a genuine humanitarian aid organisation. Aerdrie has spent four thousand years concluding this combination is the most efficient possible use of the same infrastructure.

Rillifane Rallathil — The Leaflord
God of Nature and Druids · Architect of the Grove Network
NatureDruidsWild SpacesEcological Memory

The Grove Network is Rillifane's living instrument — a distributed system of Druid circles connected through shared ecological monitoring that Circle-Speaker Verdant coordinates across the continent's wild spaces. The contamination pattern that Verdant is documenting was first identified in Rillifane's own divine perception approximately two centuries ago. He has been waiting for the right mortal — or the right collection of mortals — to identify it from below. He is still waiting. The pattern is almost complete enough to be undeniable.

Erevan Ilesere — The Chameleon
God of Mischief and Change · The Trickster of the Seldarine
MischiefChangeInformation

Erevan's mortal form as the street performer Merry Tanglewood is Aethrya's most accessible divine interaction point for Bard characters — and its most carefully calibrated one. Merry operates at a level of social and performance capability that is mechanically equivalent to a Master Bard with College of Eloquence. Merry has been shaping what stories get told, to whom, and in what form, for longer than any mortal tradition can document. What Merry is interested in hearing, right now, from the characters who share their performance space, is a question the DM team considers the most open-ended active prompt in the server's current story architecture.
