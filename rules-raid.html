Infiltration, Raid & Siege

The structured framework for organized conflict between factions, guilds, and settlements â€” from solo covert operations to the full weight of a siege declaration, with all its permanent world consequences.

Infiltration
Solo or small group (1â€“3) covert entry for intelligence, theft, or sabotage. Requires Stealth or Deception checks, no combat declaration, no advance notice required. Evidence trail rules apply in full.
Raid
Organized combat assault (2â€“6 attackers) on a player-controlled location. Requires 24-hour declaration to the target faction's leadership. 48-hour cooldown before a second raid on the same location.
Siege
Large-scale assault on a settlement or fortified position. Requires 72-hour declaration, DM oversight, minimum five attackers. Resolution is a DM-managed event with permanent world consequences.

Declaration Requirements

Raids and Sieges require a formal declaration submitted to the Moderation team and delivered IC to the target faction's leadership. Declarations must include: the attacking group, the target location, the stated objective (what constitutes victory), and the proposed time window. Staff confirm validity before the declaration period begins.

Undeclared combat against player structures is treated under the Consequence Rules as a crime rather than a structured conflict â€” NPC guard responses and faction reactions follow accordingly, rather than the structured resolution system.

Resolution

Infiltration resolution uses the skill check system. Raid resolution uses dice combat with a DM referee present. Siege resolution is a full DM event whose outcome the DM team determines based on player action, narrative context, and consequence trees prepared in advance. Siege outcomes may permanently alter faction standing, territory control, and available player options in the affected region â€” they are among the most consequential events Aethrya's player community can produce.
