The Veilbreakers

The mortal investigators who have dedicated their lives to exposing hidden divine activity — operating in isolated cells, building their files one careful observation at a time, and currently being surveilled by a hidden god who has not yet decided what to do about what they know.

Membership in the Veilbreakers makes you a target of every divine faction simultaneously. Kael Ironveil's surveillance operation began fourteen days ago. The Veilbreakers do not yet know. One of their Research Division analysts is currently in grief counselling with Velara Dusk.

Organisation Structure

The Veilbreakers operate in cells — small groups sharing only the information necessary for their specific operational scope. No single cell knows the full roster of the organisation. This structure was designed to prevent the infiltration that destroyed the predecessor organisation, the Accord Watchers, forty years ago. It works. The gods find it deeply, genuinely irritating.

Research Division
Document analysis, historical pattern-matching, theological inconsistency identification. The most intellectually demanding cell type and the most valuable. The doctoral student investigation began when a Research analyst noticed curriculum theorems predating their supposed discovery by two hundred years.
Field Division
Surveillance, evidence collection, contact cultivation. The operatives who follow the leads that Research identifies. Their methods overlap extensively with Rogue and Ranger class capabilities — not coincidentally.
Archival Division
Maintains the Veilbreaker files — dossiers on every suspected divine mortal-form identity. The Faerûnian file is the largest. The entry on Tessara Wand contains six independently sourced observations that she has not aged in thirty years of faculty service. It is marked "Unconfirmed."
Communications
Cross-cell information routing using coded channels rotating on a 7-day cycle. The organisation's greatest structural vulnerability: it requires central coordination that necessarily creates a map. The map is exactly what Kael Ironveil wants.

What They Do Not Yet Know

The Veilbreakers do not yet know: that Kael Ironveil's surveillance operations began fourteen days ago, that one of their Research analysts is in active therapy with Shar's mortal form, that the Blank Monk who has attended their last six public events is not a prospective recruit, or that their most significant open case — the Veil Accord's founding document anomaly — has already been independently identified by the Silent Synod and cross-referenced with evidence that makes it considerably more alarming than the Veilbreakers currently believe.
