Setting

World Overview

Six thousand years after the Divine War, the gods did not leave. They simply became quieter. Every institution you trust, every stranger who offers you something useful at exactly the right moment — in Aethrya, every coincidence has a divine address.

Setting

The Veillands — Geography & Regions

From the imperial heartland of the Throne of Ashes to the unmapped territories where Primordial Titans stir beneath geological anomalies that only Rangers have learned to read correctly — the living map of Aethrya's world.

Setting

Season Story Timeline

The living record of what Aethrya's players have collectively made happen — the convergence of personal arcs, faction manoeuvres, and the slow seismic pressure of something ancient beginning to wake.

Setting

Factions of Aethrya

Seven player-joinable factions whose competing agendas map directly onto the divine cold war — each offering a distinct entry point into the world's living political architecture, and each carrying threads that connect, eventually, to every other.

Systems

Languages of Aethrya

Common tongues, racial scripts, divine languages, and the forbidden utterances whose very use constitutes a detectable act under certain interpretations of the Veil Accord — the linguistic architecture of a world where words have always carried more weight than they appear to.

Systems

Magic & The Weave

Every arcane spell cast in Aethrya is drawn through a living divine framework maintained by a hidden goddess. Every divine spell is answered by a patron who is present in the mortal world, somewhere, in mortal form. Magic in Aethrya is never private. There is always someone on the other end.

Systems

Professions Overview

Every character requires a profession — the economic and social role that grounds them in Aethrya's living world beyond their class's combat and magical capabilities. Professions have their own mastery track, drive the player economy, and determine which institutional networks value your non-combat contributions.

Systems

The Wound System

How wounds are applied, tracked, treated — and why a character who survived a fight with three grievous injuries tells a richer story than one who emerged unscathed. Physical conflict in Aethrya leaves marks, and those marks are part of the narrative.

Factions

Leilon — The Neutral Shore

The coastal trade city where every faction maintains a presence and no faction holds dominance — a carefully tended armistice that functions as the server's primary starting hub and the closest approximation of neutral ground in a world defined by hidden allegiances.

Factions

Phandalin — The Frontier's Edge

The furthest permanent settlement into the unmapped Veillands — rebuilt twice, fortified against a threat it has never fully been able to name, and increasingly aware that the geological reports its expedition parties bring back describe something no existing model can explain.

Factions

Southkrypt — The In-Between City

Where the Underdark and the surface world negotiate their terms — a city built in the political gap between two civilisations, trading in discretion as readily as goods, and offering every character who arrives there a connection they did not expect and cannot fully explain.

Factions

The Veilbreakers

The mortal investigators who have dedicated their lives to exposing hidden divine activity — operating in isolated cells, building their files one careful observation at a time, and currently being surveilled by a hidden god who has not yet decided what to do about what they know.

Factions

The Silent Synod

The secretive theological research confraternity that knows more about the Veil Accord than any mortal institution should — and whose founding documents contain a preface attributed to someone who was present at the Accord's signing and has not yet decided what to do about what they know.

Factions

Order of the Hidden Flame

The Paladin order that serves divine justice without knowing which hidden god empowers them — whose founder spent fifty years trying to identify her patron and concluded the answer didn't matter. The god on the other end of their power agrees. For reasons neither party has fully disclosed.

Factions

Children of the Spider Queen

Maerath's mortal web — surface cells, Underdark chapters, and the most sophisticated intelligence operation any deity runs in the Veil era. Every transaction in their network is connected to something else. No strand knows the whole shape of what it supports.

Classes

Classes Overview

Thirteen classes — each deeply embedded in Aethrya's divine politics in ways that are not always visible at character creation. Your class does not merely describe how you fight or cast. It describes which divine network you were born adjacent to, and what that network wants from you.

Classes

Mastery Progression

The four-tier advancement system that governs every class and profession in Aethrya — not a time gate but a narrative gate. Each tier is a commitment to inhabiting your character's capabilities at their fullest expression before accessing the next level of possibility.

Classes

Fighter

The martial professional whose skill sustains every divine faction's ground operations — serving Tempus's Order of the Iron Gauntlet, Bane's Black Fist military branches, or operating as an independent contractor whose capabilities are courted by every power that needs a capable sword and cannot afford to ask where its loyalties currently lie.

Classes

Wizard

Every spell you cast is drawn through the living framework of a hidden goddess's domain. The Church of the Seven Stars' registration of your arcane talent is Tessara Wand's personal intelligence census. The doctoral thesis your advisor praised last month has three divine agents monitoring its theoretical direction.

Classes

Cleric

You are the direct conduit of a hidden god's divine power. Your temple is a mortal front for an intelligence operation. Your congregation's prayers sustain a being you have never met in their true form. Your Divine Intervention, at Expert mastery, is a DM ticket — a formal request that your god's mortal network manifest in your vicinity. They always answer in ways that maintain plausible deniability.

Classes

Paladin

Your power flows through an Oath to principles, not allegiance to a patron — the divine workaround for a world where gods cannot openly champion mortal warriors. The god who answers your Oath may not be the one you assumed. Your conviction, not your assumption, is the point.

Classes

Rogue

In a world built on hidden agendas and carefully constructed ignorance, your skill set is not merely useful — it is the primary challenge every divine network must solve before it can operate safely. The Veilbreakers recruit heavily from your tradition. So does the Children of the Spider Queen. Both know why.

Classes

Ranger

The wilderness professionals who encounter Primordial Titan evidence first, who read the frontier's anomalies before any institution has catalogued them, and who stand at the threshold of the season's most significant revelation — if they recognise what the pattern means before it recognises them.

Classes

Bard

The information economy of Aethryan civilisation — the profession that every divine network simultaneously cultivates and fears. In a world where four thousand years of divine manipulation has required meticulous management of collective memory, the Bard who asks the wrong questions in the right venue is more dangerous than the Paladin who draws a sword.

Classes

Druid

Your connection to natural forces predates the Veil Accord's entire organised theological structure — which makes you simultaneously the most independent caster on the server and the most coveted asset of every divine faction that manages its intelligence through institutional religion. Rillifane's Grove Network has a contamination map with four data points. You may become the fifth.

Classes

Warlock

Your power comes from a negotiated agreement. In a world where the most powerful entities available for such arrangements include hidden gods operating in mortal disguise, the question of who you actually bargained with carries implications that grow more urgent at every mastery tier. What does your patron want? The answer changes when the Titan pattern becomes visible.

Classes

Monk

Your power has no obvious divine sponsor — developed through internal cultivation traditions that no single divine faction can claim as its institutional product. In Aethrya, this means you are either genuinely autonomous — a possibility that delights Tymora and troubles Bane — or very carefully disguised. The divine factions have not agreed on which. Your arc is the living argument.

Classes

Sorcerer

Your magic flows from within — from bloodline, from cosmic circumstance, from proximity to forces whose scale you may never fully comprehend. Mystra and Shar have both written research papers on what it means that divine power can bleed into mortal bloodlines through centuries of proximity to hidden gods. Neither has published their findings. Both have noted your name.

Classes

Artificer

The magical engineers who understand that the boundary between magic and technology is methodology, not fundamental distinction. Galdrick Sparkwit's divine-detection device is Artificer-class work built by Flan Ironpocket. Both are hidden gods. An Artificer who seeks mentorship from either enters the most productive workshop — and the most closely watched — available in Aethrya.

Classes

Barbarian

Your Rage accesses primal power that the Veil Accord's provisions were never written to govern. The Judicators have never formally adjudicated the gap this creates. A Zealot Barbarian who investigates the true source of their divine Rage will find that the answer traces to something older and stranger than any recognised pantheon — and that the Primordial Titan awakening arc runs directly through them.

Races

Races of Aethrya

Forty-seven playable races across three availability tiers — each carrying their own cultural history, divine entanglements, and established role within the world's political and ecological structure. Your race determines your initial relationship to Aethrya's divine politics before your first scene opens.

Races

Fabled Race Expectations

The elevated standards, behavioural commitments, and community responsibilities that define the Fabled designation — not as restrictions imposed upon you, but as the architecture that transforms the most extraordinary character options into genuine narrative forces within the living world.

Races

Common Races

The thirteen races available as standard whitelist options — each carrying their own cultural history, divine entanglements, and place within Aethrya's living world. Every common race is embedded in at least one divine network's operational strategy, whether or not their players are yet aware of it.

Races

Limited Races

Races available in restricted quantities — their scarcity within the server's population reflecting their scarcity within the world itself. Limited races carry elevated narrative visibility: a Drow in Leilon, a Hengeyokai in the Throne of Ashes, are noticed in ways a human or elf is not, and that visibility is both a story asset and a story challenge.

Races

Fabled Races

The most extraordinary character options in Aethrya — including the Pantheon Race, which allows a player to embody a hidden god themselves. Each Fabled race represents a categorical shift in the kind of story a character tells and the weight of responsibility that character carries within the world.

Pantheons

Pantheons of Aethrya

Seven divine assemblies maintaining mortal operations across the known world — from the Faerûnian Pantheon's institutional empire to the Primordial Titans who predate every theology that claims to explain them. The divine cold war is not between good and evil. It is between agendas that have had four thousand years to become sophisticated.

Pantheons

The Pantheon Race

The most extraordinary character option in Aethrya — the ability to play a hidden god in mortal form. Subject to the Veil Accord's full constraints, the Judicators' ongoing monitoring, and the extraordinary dramatic pressure of maintaining a perfect disguise before a world that is slowly becoming more capable of seeing through it.

Pantheons

The Faerûnian Pantheon — Full Roster

Not a unified church but a coalition of sovereign deities with overlapping domains, individual theological traditions, and a four-thousand-year history of rivalry conducted through the most elaborate system of institutional proxies ever assembled. Every Faerûnian institution you trust is managed by a being you have never seen in their true form.

Pantheons

The Seldarine — The Elven Court

A court rather than a coalition — bound by kinship, shared history, and the Long Memory tradition that preserves theological truth across timescales no human institution survives intact. The Seldarine's divine cold war strategy is patience itself: elves remember what mortal civilisations forget, and in Aethrya, memory is the most dangerous weapon of all.

Pantheons

The Dark Seldarine — The Drow Pantheon

The divine assembly governing the Sundered — the Drow who chose or were driven into the deep places. Unlike the Faerûnian Pantheon, which manages mortal ignorance of divine presence, the Dark Seldarine governs a civilisation where divine manipulation is not a hidden background assumption but an acknowledged, expected, and celebrated element of daily political life.

Pantheons

The Morndinsamman — The Dwarven Pantheon

The divine family of the dwarven peoples — whose forge-temple institutions have shaped dwarven civilisation for longer than the Veil Accord has existed, whose divine presences in the Irondeep are the most institutionally embedded of any pantheon in Aethrya, and whose relationship to the Primordial Titan awakening carries theological implications none of them have fully addressed aloud.

Pantheons

Yondalla's Children — The Halfling Pantheon

The divine family of the halfling peoples — whose mortal strategy of quiet protectiveness, institutional invisibility, and deliberate cultivation of being underestimated has produced results that would astonish every faction that has ever moved against a halfling community and found the response more coordinated than the attacker anticipated.

Pantheons

Lords of the Golden Hills — The Gnomish Pantheon

The divine family whose engineering, wit, and foundational theological curiosity have produced the season's most strategically significant technological development: a device that detects divine presence. Operational. In mortal hands. Functional at forty feet. Currently being extended. Every deity in Aethrya has an opinion about this. None has formally acted.

Pantheons

The Primordial Titans — Before the Gods

The ancient presences that predate every pantheon, every accord, every theological tradition in the known world — not gods in any recognisable sense, not bound by any covenant, and currently expressing themselves through geological events that four data points in the Grove Network's contamination map are beginning to make terrifyingly legible.

Spells & Rituals

Spells Overview

Aethrya's full spell system — organised by tier from Cantrip to Master, with a ritual system that connects magical practice to divine institutional networks in ways that even experienced spellcasters may not have fully accounted for.

Spells & Rituals

Novice Tier Spells

The foundational threads of magical practice — 1st and 2nd level spells that represent a spellcaster's first deliberate, controlled engagement with Mystra's living Weave. Every cantrip is an unremarkable thread pulled from a goddess's framework. In Aethrya, even the unremarkable is observed.

Spells & Rituals

Adept Tier Spells

Third and fourth level spells — where the Weave's capabilities begin asserting themselves in ways that draw divine attention more consistently. The tier at which most experienced spellcasters spend the majority of their active careers, and the tier at which the consequences of magical choices become genuinely difficult to contain.

Spells & Rituals

Expert Tier Spells

Fifth and sixth level spells — where the scale of magical possibility begins reshaping the world itself rather than simply affecting individuals within it. At Expert tier, a caster's choices carry consequences that extend well beyond the immediate scene, and the divine attention those choices attract is proportionally more focused.

Spells & Rituals

Master Tier Spells

Seventh, eighth, and ninth level spells — magic at the scale of legend, where each casting reshapes reality in ways the world cannot ignore and divine networks cannot afford to overlook. Master-tier spellcasting in Aethrya is not merely powerful. It is geopolitically significant.

Spells & Rituals

Rituals

The extended magical workings that require time rather than power — a different mode of spellcasting that trades immediacy for depth, and whose products often outlast any single combat encounter by days, weeks, or, in the case of certain divine-adjacent rituals, centuries.
