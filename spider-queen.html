Children of the Spider Queen

Maerath's mortal web — surface cells, Underdark chapters, and the most sophisticated intelligence operation any deity runs in the Veil era. Every transaction in their network is connected to something else. No strand knows the whole shape of what it supports.

Overview

The Children are organised as a spider's web — a central node connected to peripheral cells by lines no single cell can trace to their full extent. Maerath's mortal form does not directly command the Children; she manages the web's architecture, ensuring information flowing through it serves her purposes without any strand knowing what shape the whole makes. Membership ranges from true believers in Maerath's theological vision to mercenary contractors who know only their specific assignment and their handler's designation. The organisation works because it does not need its members to understand it — only to do their part.

The Godslayer Blade

The Children's current primary operational focus is an artifact of the Divine War era whose theological implications are significant enough that multiple divine factions maintain standing operations to locate it. The Children believe it is in the eastern Veillands, based on a pattern of evidence accumulated over thirty years. Three other factions believe it is somewhere else. At least one of those factions is correct. The Children are methodical enough that their location might still be right. The season arc will determine which.

Surface Operations

Leilon — A trading house specialising in rare textile imports. Its back-room conversations are considerably more interesting than the inventory suggests.

Emberreach — A financial consulting firm whose client list overlaps with the Interior Ministry's surveillance targets in a way that neither party has yet noticed.

Southkrypt — Three simultaneous legitimate businesses, two of which the Merchant Council has officially declined to investigate. The third is the interesting one.

Surface cell members generally do not know they serve Maerath directly. They know they work for an organisation that values discretion, pays reliably, and makes requests that always connect to something significant about six months after they fulfilled them. The discovery of who they actually serve — available as a DM arc at Adept mastery — is one of the most carefully designed narrative revelations in Aethrya. It is not a surprise that ends a story. It is a surprise that begins one.
