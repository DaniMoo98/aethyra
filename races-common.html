Races

Common Races

The thirteen races available as standard whitelist options — each carrying their own cultural history, divine entanglements, and place within Aethrya's living world. Every common race is embedded in at least one divine network's operational strategy, whether or not their players are yet aware of it.

Human
The most numerous mortal race — adaptable, politically dominant in the Throne of Ashes, and the primary target of every Faerûnian Pantheon institution's influence operations. Every major divine agenda is expressed primarily through human institutional structures. Humans are, in a meaningful sense, the medium through which the divine cold war is conducted.
Mountain Dwarf
The warrior-caste dwarves of the upper Irondeep — stoic, honour-bound, deeply loyal to clan and Moradin. Their forge-temple tradition sustains the most intimate mortal-divine connection in Aethrya. Durn Anvilhand's professional assessments carry weight in every Mountain Dwarf hold, whether or not anyone present understands why.
Gold Dwarf
The trade-focused dwarves of the Deep Holds — masterful craftspeople and cautious of outsiders in the particular way that comes from having seen four thousand years of outsiders prove that caution correct. Vergadain's commercial intelligence network runs through Gold Dwarf merchant families in ways that the families themselves generally regard as exceptional business acumen.
Moon Elf
The most socially engaged of elven subraces — curious, traveller-inclined, and deeply tied to the Long Memory tradition transmitted through Aelindra's worship. Their cultural instinct toward genuine connection with other peoples makes them naturally effective in the intelligence-gathering operations that multiple divine factions rely on. This is not a coincidence.
Wood Elf
Forest-dwelling elves of the Veillands border regions — agile, reclusive, and the primary practitioners of Rillifane's Grove tradition. Their territorial knowledge of the frontier regions makes them the most valuable characters for the early stages of the Titan awakening investigation. What they have been seeing at the edges of their territory for the last three years is in the Grove Network's files.
Sun Elf
The aristocratic elves of ancient Silvenmoor — proud keepers of pre-Veil knowledge, closely aligned with Corellon's Preservation Circles. Their Long Memory spans the full four thousand years of the Veil era. A Sun Elf Scholar character who commits to the Preservation Circles' research is working with documents that no other race's institutional tradition has maintained.
Half-Elf
Children of human-elven unions — caught between two Long Memories, prized as diplomats and intelligence operatives by factions that require fluency in both cultural frameworks. Every major power maintains Half-Elf figures in positions that require navigating the boundary between human political structures and elven institutional depth.
Halfling
Small, resilient, community-focused wanderers — quietly protected by Yondalla's accumulated goodwill network and profoundly underestimated by every faction that has ever moved against a halfling community. The cost of that underestimation is documented in the Veilbreakers' oldest case files and nowhere else.
Gnome
Forest Gnome variant — fey-touched illusionists and naturalists with an instinctive theological curiosity that makes them natural, if typically unwitting, investigators of divine activity. The gnomish tradition of taking things apart to understand how they work does not stop at mechanical devices. Garl Glittergold's mortal form represents where this instinct leads when it has four thousand years and divine patience to work with.
Dragonborn
Draconic-heritage warriors whose ancient compact with chromatic and metallic dragons predates the Veil Accord and gives them a unique position in divine politics: an existing non-human institutional framework that the Accord's provisions were not designed to fully accommodate. The Judicators have a standing advisory on the Dragonborn question that has been "under review" for nine hundred years.
Half-Orc
Born of human-orc lineage — physically powerful, socially marginalised, and historically associated with the frontier's Malicious race populations. Their position between two communities that have historically been in conflict gives them a perspective on Aethrya's political landscape that neither community's institutional frameworks fully accounts for. This perspective has value that most institutions have not yet recognised, which is itself instructive.
Goliath
Giant-descended mountain peoples — tribal, honour-bound, and occupying territories that border several known Primordial Titan manifestation sites. Their oral tradition includes descriptions of geological behaviour that their elders dismiss as myth and that the Grove Network's contamination files describe as consistent primary-source documentation.
Duergar
Grey dwarves of the deep Underdark — psychically capable, fiercely isolationist, and operating under a theological framework entirely separate from the Morndinsamman. Their independence from the standard dwarven divine network makes them simultaneously the hardest dwarves to influence through established channels and the most interesting to every faction that finds standard channels insufficient.
