Spells & Rituals

Adept Tier Spells

Third and fourth level spells — where the Weave's capabilities begin asserting themselves in ways that draw divine attention more consistently. The tier at which most experienced spellcasters spend the majority of their active careers, and the tier at which the consequences of magical choices become genuinely difficult to contain.

Counterspell
AbjurationAdeptWizard · Sorcerer · Warlock
Interrupt and cancel a spell as it is cast. The tactical pivot point in any encounter involving multiple spellcasters. In divine-political terms, a Counterspell that successfully interrupts what turns out to be a divine agent's working creates a documentation trail — Veilbreaker Field Division operatives are specifically trained to recognise the signature of Counterspell interactions with divine-origin magic, which produces a different resonance pattern than standard arcane interruption.
Fireball
EvocationAdeptWizard · Sorcerer
8d6 fire damage in a 20-foot radius sphere. The most recognisable combat spell in Aethrya's magical tradition, and the one most reliably productive of collateral damage, witness reports, and investigative interest. Hub city use is restricted. Frontier deployment carries consequence risk proportional to what occupied the blast radius at the moment of detonation.
Dispel Magic
AbjurationAdeptAll Full Casters
End magical effects on a target creature, object, or point in space. Cannot affect divine disguises, which are sustained by divine will rather than Weave-mediated magic. This limitation is documented in the Silent Synod's research as one of the primary empirical distinctions between divine and arcane magical effects — a distinction that most theological institutions have conspicuously declined to incorporate into their formal curricula.
Hypnotic Pattern
IllusionAdeptWizard · Sorcerer · Bard · Warlock
A twisting pattern of colours fills a 30-foot cube, incapacitating creatures who fail their Wisdom save. Subject to Charm and Dominate Rules consent framework for player targets. One of the spells the Shar research division has documented as used by Darkness cell operatives during what the cells describe as "grief processing sessions." The documentation is not complete.
Haste
TransmutationAdeptWizard · Sorcerer
One willing creature doubles in speed, gains +2 AC, advantage on Dexterity saves, and an additional action each turn. Concentration, one minute. The most significant single-target combat enhancement available at Adept tier — and the spell whose concentration loss at the wrong moment produces the most dramatically unfortunate results of any spell on the server.
Spirit Guardians
ConjurationAdeptCleric
Summon spectral guardians that slow and damage enemies within 15 feet. Concentration. The primary reason Clerics are effective in extended combat situations — continuous area denial and damage that compounds with every round maintained. Doomguide Order combat doctrine is built around this spell at the Adept tier, and Casker Morvaine's coroner work means he encounters applications of it he did not expect to see.
Leomund's Tiny Hut
EvocationAdept (Ritual)Wizard · Bard
Create a dome of magical force for 8 hours that protects occupants from the elements and prevents entry by creatures outside. Ritual casting only. The spell that makes wilderness travel genuinely survivable for caster-heavy groups — and the spell that creates a legally interesting question about whether a conversation conducted inside a Tiny Hut is admissible as evidence in Aldrus Cole's court, a question that has not yet been formally tested.
