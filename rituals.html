Spells & Rituals

Rituals

The extended magical workings that require time rather than power — a different mode of spellcasting that trades immediacy for depth, and whose products often outlast any single combat encounter by days, weeks, or, in the case of certain divine-adjacent rituals, centuries.

Ritual spells require 10 additional minutes of casting time but do not expend Spell Points when cast in this extended form. They represent careful, deliberate magical work rather than combat deployment — and in Aethrya, the distinction between careful deliberate magic and immediate force is often the distinction between a character who shapes the world and one who merely reacts to it.

Divine rituals — those specific to religious practice and performed within institutional frameworks — carry additional layers of significance. When a Cleric of Kelemvor performs a death rite, Casker Morvaine is aware of it, in some sense, in a way that is difficult to categorise within standard magical taxonomy. When a Druid of Rillifane's network performs the Grove Renewal, Circle-Speaker Verdant's contamination map updates. Rituals in Aethrya are not merely magical procedures — they are the threads of a divine attention network that has been monitoring mortal magical practice for four thousand years.

Certain ritual workings are prohibited under the Veil Accord's secondary provisions — specifically those designed to pierce the divine disguise, compel divine revelation, or communicate directly with a deity without going through their institutional mortal network. These rituals exist. They are documented in the Silent Synod's archives. Their use constitutes one of the few acts that will reliably produce a Judicator response within 24 hours of completion. The Veilbreakers have attempted two of them. Both attempts produced the same result: the ritual completed, produced data, and the data was incomplete in the same specific way. The Synod's annotation on this pattern is three words: "the gap persists."
