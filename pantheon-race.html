Pantheons

The Pantheon Race

The most extraordinary character option in Aethrya — the ability to play a hidden god in mortal form. Subject to the Veil Accord's full constraints, the Judicators' ongoing monitoring, and the extraordinary dramatic pressure of maintaining a perfect disguise before a world that is slowly becoming more capable of seeing through it.

The Pantheon Race requires the complete Fabled application process: written backstory, Lore Team interview, designated contact, and onboarding arc documentation. It is the highest-demand Fabled option and the one the Lore Team evaluates most carefully. The quality of the application determines the depth of the arc prepared for the character.

The Veil Accord's Constraints

A Pantheon Race character is a deity operating under the Veil Accord's full provisions. Their divine power is real — but its expression is constrained to what can pass as mortal capability. They may not perform miracles. They may not reveal their divine nature. They may not exercise power in ways that would be classified as miraculous by an observant mortal. The Judicators monitor known divine mortal-form operations continuously. Any Accord violation triggers a Judicator response within 24 hours.

What remains is still extraordinary. A Pantheon Race character has access to divine perception — the awareness of their domain's activities across the world. They maintain connections to their divine institutional network. They have the patience and planning horizon of an immortal being. They are, in every scene they occupy, the most strategically sophisticated individual present — even when they are performing the role of an ordinary person with perfect conviction.

Mastery — God Mastery Tiers

Pantheon Race characters use the standard Mastery system but with content specific to their divine portfolio. Each tier unlocks additional access to divine perception, institutional network depth, and carefully constrained expressions of divine capability that remain within Accord limits. Master tier — the highest — places a Pantheon Race character at the threshold of Accord-limit capabilities. What they choose to do at that edge is the defining question of their arc.

MasteryDivine AccessConstraint Level
NoviceBasic domain awareness; mortal-level capabilitiesFull Accord provisions apply
AdeptInstitutional network access; domain-adjacent perception within 1 mileMiracle threshold strictly enforced
ExpertExpanded domain expression; divine communication with portfolio entitiesJudicator monitoring intensifies — any Accord adjacency triggers review
MasterNear-Accord-limit capabilities; direct network command; world-shaping domain expressions within constraintsEvery significant action reviewed by Judicators. The Accord's edge is the character's permanent address.

The Pantheon Race's central dramatic question is not "how much power can I use" — it is "how long can I maintain this?" A god in mortal form is playing a role that cannot slip. Not in a crisis. Not under pressure. Not when someone you care about is at risk and you could simply stop all of this if you would just be what you actually are. The Veil Accord is not a mechanical limit. It is a four-thousand-year commitment to never breaking character, and the most compelling Pantheon Race arcs are the ones where maintaining it costs the character something real.
