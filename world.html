Aethrya is a world where the gods lost their war, accepted a treaty, and chose to remain. Not as distant patrons issuing guidance through celestial intermediaries â€” as people. Walking the same streets. Eating at the same inns. Teaching at the same colleges, running the same guilds, sitting on the same courts. Because the questions they are asking, individually and collectively, are starting to assemble into something that cannot be explained any other way.

The Three Questions

Every character in Aethrya is living inside three questions, whether they know it or not. The arc of a character's life on the server is, in one meaningful sense, the arc of their engagement with these questions.

Who is walking beside you? The divine cold war is conducted through mortal institutions. Every significant NPC has a chain of authority that connects, at some remove, to a hidden god. The question of which hidden god, and what they want, is the master investigative question of the server.

What does the pattern mean? The geological anomalies, the Grove Network contamination map, the coordinated animal behaviour at the frontier, the seismic events in Phandalin â€” these are not separate phenomena. When a character with sufficient investigation capacity assembles them correctly, the shape they form triggers the season's defining narrative event.

What will you do when you know? Every faction, every divine agent, every Judicator file is ultimately waiting for the answer to this question. In Aethrya, knowledge is never its own destination. What you do with what you learn is the only thing that matters.
