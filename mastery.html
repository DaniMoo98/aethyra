Mastery Progression

The four-tier advancement system that governs every class and profession in Aethrya — not a time gate but a narrative gate. Each tier is a commitment to inhabiting your character's capabilities at their fullest expression before accessing the next level of possibility.

Novice
Entry
The foundation. Character creation tier. Basic class features, starting spell access, foundational faction relationships. Where every story begins.
Adept
Tier 2
Demonstrable competence. Subclass features, expanded spell tiers, deeper faction integration. Requires 3 attended DM sessions and DM team assessment of roleplay quality.
Expert
Tier 3
Class mastery — capabilities that reshape encounters and investigations. Requires participation in a major narrative arc and DM confirmation that Expert-tier behaviour has been demonstrated.
Master
Tier 4
World-shaping capability. The highest expression of your class. DM interview, documented mentorship of Novice characters, and the understanding that Master characters cannot move unnoticed.

What Mastery Really Means

Mastery in Aethrya is not a reward for time spent. It is a recognition that the character has earned their capabilities through demonstrated excellence in the roleplay that those capabilities require. A Novice Cleric who has attended three sessions and been present does not automatically advance to Adept — they advance when the DM team's observation of their roleplay confirms that they understand what being an Adept Cleric means, both mechanically and narratively.

This standard exists because of the world Aethrya is. When an Expert character uses Divine Intervention, the world responds through DM event architecture. When a Master character acts, every divine faction that monitors that character class has a protocol for it. The mastery system is the guarantee that these world-shaping capabilities are in the hands of players who understand the responsibility they carry — and who will use them to make the world's story richer rather than simply more complicated.
