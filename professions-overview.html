Professions Overview

Every character requires a profession — the economic and social role that grounds them in Aethrya's living world beyond their class's combat and magical capabilities. Professions have their own mastery track, drive the player economy, and determine which institutional networks value your non-combat contributions.

Professions are not secondary to class — they are the part of a character's life that classes do not cover. A Master Wizard who cannot sustain themselves economically is dependent on factional patronage. A Novice Fighter with Master-level Blacksmith skills has resources, institutional connections, and economic leverage that their class alone cannot provide. The two tracks advance independently, and the combination of class mastery and profession mastery defines a character's full capability range more completely than either track alone.

Blacksmith
Weapons, armour, tools. The forge tradition of Moradin's Irondeep — producing the equipment that every other profession and class ultimately depends upon.
Alchemist
Potions, poisons, reagents. The server's most consumed profession output — healing supplies, buff consumables, and experimental compounds at Master tier.
Merchant
Trade, appraisal, commerce. From market stall to cross-faction economic diplomacy — Vergadain's professional domain expressed through mortal enterprise.
Scholar
Research, history, investigation. The most dangerous profession in a world where collective memory has been managed by hidden gods for four thousand years.
Hunter
Tracking, trapping, harvesting. The frontier's primary early-warning system for the Titan awakening pattern — if the character knows what to look for.
Quartermaster
Logistics, bulk supply, faction operations. The economic leverage that comes from controlling what flows where — and the ability to redirect it.
Prospector
Geological surveying, mineral assessment. First to document the subsonic vibration patterns at Titan sites — before anyone knew what they were measuring.
Woodsman
Lumber, forestry, territorial knowledge. Grove Network adjacent — their working territory overlaps directly with the contamination mapping operation.
Forager
Botanical gathering, wilderness survival. Herbalist-adjacent — documenting the accelerated growth and die-off cycles that mark Titan proximity.
Cartographer
Map-making, territorial documentation. The profession that turns investigation into permanence — and whose work the Veilbreakers' Field Division uses more than any other profession's output.
