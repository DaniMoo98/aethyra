Feedback & Suggestions

Aethrya is a living collaborative creation — the DM team's frameworks, the lore team's world, and the community's evolving story are all shaped by what you experience as a player. Your perspective is not incidental; it is part of the process by which the server improves.

We actively seek feedback on all dimensions of the Aethrya experience: rules clarity, lore consistency, event pacing, faction balance, profession economy, NPC encounter quality, and anything else that shapes your experience of the world. You cannot break the server by giving honest feedback. You can make it better.

Feedback Channels

📋
#general-feedback
Open-channel feedback on any aspect of the server. Public and visible to the full staff team. The best channel for suggestions you'd like community discussion around.
🎲
#rules-questions
Rules clarification requests and edge case questions. Staff responses here are treated as official rulings for the scenario described. The channel's history functions as Aethrya's living FAQ.
🌍
#lore-suggestions
World-building suggestions, lore questions, and proposals for how your character's arc could connect to the server's larger story. The Lore Team reads every submission in this channel.
🔒
Staff Ticket
Private feedback on sensitive matters — player conduct concerns, personal safety issues, or feedback about a specific staff member's handling of a situation. All tickets are reviewed by senior staff.

What We Do With Feedback

Every substantive piece of feedback enters a review process. Rules suggestions are discussed in weekly staff meetings. Lore suggestions are evaluated against the established world framework and, when compatible and compelling, integrated into the next appropriate story beat. Player conduct feedback is actioned through the moderation pipeline with appropriate confidentiality. You will receive a response to every ticket, typically within 48 hours.

The server's most significant improvements — the wound system's recovery curve adjustment, the Fabled application process redesign, the Charm Rules consent framework expansion — all began as player feedback submissions. The world you inhabit tomorrow is being shaped by what you share with us today.
