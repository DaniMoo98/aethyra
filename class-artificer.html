Artificer

The magical engineers who understand that the boundary between magic and technology is methodology, not fundamental distinction. Galdrick Sparkwit's divine-detection device is Artificer-class work built by Flan Ironpocket. Both are hidden gods. An Artificer who seeks mentorship from either enters the most productive workshop — and the most closely watched — available in Aethrya.

Setting Role & the Gnomish Tradition

Artificers operate where Garl Glittergold's Clockwork Ceremony — the gnomish theological tradition of sacred engineering — meets Mystra's Imperial College network. The Infusion system allows creation of semi-permanent magical enhancements to mundane objects, producing a sustained supply of magical items that no other class can maintain. At Expert mastery, the Spell-Storing Item allows a non-spellcaster to cast a stored spell without magical ability — a mechanical transfer of divine or arcane capability that faction supply systems across Aethrya actively seek to contract for.

Galdrick Sparkwit's divine-presence detection device — currently functional at forty feet, being extended with Flan Ironpocket's fabrication expertise — is the season's defining Artificer-class engineering achievement. The device's strategic implications are understood by every deity in Aethrya. None of them have formally moved to stop it. An Artificer character who investigates, assists, or builds upon this research will find themselves at the centre of the most significant technological development in Aethrya's current story arc, with every divine faction watching their progress with careful, unacknowledged attention.

Master Artificers are expected to produce original magical research or devices, document a significant application of their craft in a DM event, and demonstrate the engineering discipline that distinguishes genuine Artificer mastery from mere magical item accumulation. The bar is the bar that Galdrick and Flan set by example — which is extraordinarily high, because both of them are gods.
