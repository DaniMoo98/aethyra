Classes Overview

Thirteen classes — each deeply embedded in Aethrya's divine politics in ways that are not always visible at character creation. Your class does not merely describe how you fight or cast. It describes which divine network you were born adjacent to, and what that network wants from you.

In Aethrya, class choice has institutional consequences. A Wizard is registered with Tessara Wand's Church of the Seven Stars the moment their talent manifests. A Cleric's training is provided by a divine institution whose ultimate leadership answers to a hidden god. A Paladin's Oath channels power from a source they may never have formally identified. Even a Rogue — ostensibly the least institutionally entangled class — operates in an information economy that every divine network actively cultivates and monitors.

⚔️
Fighter
The martial professional — serving Tempus's Order of the Iron Gauntlet, Bane's Black Fist military branches, or operating as an independent contractor whose skills every faction covets.
✨
Wizard
Systematic arcane mastery — every spell drawn through Mystra's living Weave, every caster in Tessara Wand's registration files, every advanced thesis under three divine agents' simultaneous monitoring.
⛪
Cleric
The divine conduit — directly embedded in a hidden god's institutional infrastructure, whether they know it or not. Divine Intervention at Expert is a DM ticket to your patron's mortal-form network.
🛡️
Paladin
Oath-bound divine warrior — power flowing through commitment to principles rather than a named patron, which is exactly the kind of theological loophole a careful god designed four thousand years ago.
🗡️
Rogue
The information professional — Expertise, Reliable Talent, and the Impostor feature that approaches divine disguise quality. Every faction recruits heavily from this tradition for exactly the same reasons.
🌲
Ranger
The wilderness expert — first to encounter Titan awakening evidence, essential to the Grove Network's contamination mapping, and positioned at the threshold of the season's most significant revelation.
🎵
Bard
The information economy — Bardic Inspiration, the broadest skill platform, and the awareness that Erevan Ilesere's mortal form is listening to what stories are being told right now.
🌿
Druid
Pre-institutional nature connection — independent of every divine network's formal structure, which makes Druids both uniquely free and uniquely valuable to the Grove Network's ongoing work.
🌀
Warlock
The negotiated contract — power through agreement with an entity whose full nature you may never determine. In Aethrya, the entities available for such agreements include hidden gods.
🥋
Monk
Internal cultivation — no obvious divine sponsor, which every faction finds suspicious in exactly the ways that make a Monk genuinely interesting to sit across a table from.
💫
Sorcerer
Innate power — Metamagic, Subtle Spell, and the Divine Soul's theological anomaly that Mystra and Shar have both written papers about without sharing their findings with each other.
⚙️
Artificer
Engineering as magic — Infusions, the Spell-Storing Item, and the workshop mentorship of two hidden gods whose detection device is the season's most strategically consequential object.
🔥
Barbarian
Primal force — Rage accessing power the Veil Accord was never written to govern, and the Zealot thread that runs directly into the Primordial Titan awakening arc.

All classes use the Novice → Adept → Expert → Master progression. Each tier unlocks new mechanical capabilities, new story threads, and new visibility — at Master, you are a world-shaping figure who cannot easily move through Aethrya unobserved. See Mastery Progression for the full advancement requirements.
